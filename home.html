Frequently Asked Questions

Hey, I'm new to this whole wireless thing. What's the deal with Bluetooth vs. Wi-Fi?
Think of Bluetooth as your personal bubble, it's for connecting devices directly to each other over short distances. Wi-Fi is more like a neighborhood network, connecting your devices to the internet and each other over a wider area. Both are wireless, but they serve different purposes.
My wireless headphones keep cutting out. What gives?
A few things could be causing this. Are you near any other wireless devices? They might be causing interference. Try moving away from them. Also, check if your headphones need a charge or if there's a firmware update available. If all else fails, try the old "turn it off and on again" trick - it works more often than you'd think!
I'm worried about security with wireless devices. Should I be?
While wireless devices can be vulnerable, there are ways to stay safe. Keep your firmware updated, and when possible, use WPA3 encryption for your Wi-Fi. It's like locking your doors basically.
What's this 'latency' thing I keep hearing about with wireless gaming mice?
It's basically the delay between when you click your mouse and when your computer registers it. Wired mice typically have lower latency, but wireless tech has come a long way. Many modern wireless gaming mice have latency so low you won't notice a difference. Just look for mice that advertise low latency or "1ms response time."
How long should the battery in my wireless keyboard last?
It varies a lot depending on the keyboard and how you use it. Some can last months on a single charge, while others might need charging every couple of weeks. If you're using a keyboard with backlighting or lots of extra features, expect to charge more often. Pro tip: many wireless keyboards these days use rechargeable batteries, which is way more convenient than constantly swapping out AAs!
